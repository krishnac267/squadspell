How to play SquadSpell

Goal

Score the most points by playing words on the board. Each letter has a point value (shown on your tiles). Premium squares multiply letters or whole words.

Your turn

  • Tap a letter on your rack, then tap an empty square to place it. Build one row or one column this turn — no gaps in the line you’re filling.
  • Every new word must touch a tile already on the board (except the very first word).
  • Play locks your words and ends your turn. Recall pulls back tiles you placed this turn before you play.
  • Shuffle only reorders your rack — it doesn’t use your turn.

First word

Must cover the center star and be at least two letters long.

Board bonuses

  • Double / triple letter — multiplies that new tile’s value.
  • Double / triple word — multiplies the entire word’s score (after letter math).
  • The center star acts as a double word on the opening play.

Special tiles

  • Blank (?) — worth 0; when you place it, you choose which letter it stands for.
  • Mystic styles (wood, marble, cosmic) are looks only — they don’t change scoring or rules.

Words & directions

Words are read along a row or column. SquadSpell can treat a line as left‑to‑right, right‑to‑left, or vertical — the game checks valid dictionary words for the way your tiles spell.

Dictionary (Words button)

With Web, plays are checked against a built‑in starter list and an online dictionary. With Off, any word is allowed. In Wi‑Fi games, the host’s choice applies to everyone.

Full rack bonus

Place all 7 letters in one turn for a +50 bonus.

Exchange & pass

  • Exchange — swap chosen tiles with the bag; your turn ends. Needs tiles left in the bag.
  • Pass — skip placing. If everyone passes around twice in a row, the game can end early.

End of game

When someone plays their last tile and the bag is empty, the game ends. Points left on racks are subtracted; in classic style, those points go to the player who went out.

Pass & play vs Wi‑Fi

  • Pass & play — one device; only the current player’s rack shows letters. Others are blurred.
  • Wi‑Fi — each person sees only their own rack; friends need the same room code on the same network.

Tip: open the words tab on the side of the board anytime to see what was played and learn definitions when online.
